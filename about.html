About Me

Systems Engineer

I design and build systems, from low-level performance to GitOps. The red thread across my work is that I dig to the bottom of the system, deeper than the task asked for, and solve the part others gave up on. Boids at 35,000 agents and 60 FPS through spatial hashing and Unity DOTS. 3D in MonoGame when people said it could not be done. A whole SaaS built and run in production, solo.

I am not someone who uses AI to type a bit faster. I build systems, and AI is one of my tools in that. I direct an AI as my lead developer and own every architectural decision myself. MyFoodBudget is the proof that this way of working ships real, running software.

How I work

  • Fix the cause, not the symptom: when numbers disagreed across MyFoodBudget, I moved all computation behind one authoritative endpoint instead of patching mismatches one at a time.
  • Infrastructure as Code: GitOps with Flux and Kustomize, sealed-secrets so credentials are safe to commit, automated promotion through staging and production.
  • Prove, don't promise: I show running systems, not slideware. Two Kubernetes clusters, a CI pipeline, monitoring, and encrypted nightly backups, all live.
  • Depth before language: I understand the principles behind good code, so a stack I have not used yet is a short ramp, not a wall.

How I got here

DevOps was not a career box I ticked. During a course a teacher mentioned, in passing, that he hosted his own things in containers and ran a homelab. I went home, looked it up, and found it was exactly my thing. At the exam I used Watchtower to auto-update images, and the teacher took a note for his own homelab because he had not heard of it. From there I went hard on it in my own time and carried it into every project since.

MyFoodBudget

I built MyFoodBudget because I needed the tool myself. It started as my internship and bachelor thesis and just kept growing. Along the way I found out other families could use it too. It is something I am passionate about finishing and releasing as v1. It runs in production on my own infrastructure today.

Beyond software

I am not a 22 year old who only knows code. I have lived, led, and worked internationally, and I chose software as my next chapter with open eyes. That background is not a side story next to the tech. It made me better at it.

  • Leadership, for real: sergeant in armoured infantry, training and commanding soldiers under pressure. When I was Scrum Master and lead in my internship, I drew on having actually led people before.
  • A whole career in agriculture and sustainability: trained as a farmer, ran permaculture startup projects in South Africa and Guatemala, built with Earthship sustainable construction, and chaired Earthship Denmark. Designing robust, self-sustaining systems is the same instinct whether it is a farm or a backend.
  • Work under pressure: years as a chef in a busy kitchen taught me time management and staying calm when everything is on fire at once.
  • Always learning more: Harvard's CS50 and CS50P online, on top of the degree, because I wanted to.

It connects to the work in concrete ways. My agriculture and permaculture knowledge fed straight into the KlimaPark learning game, where I could talk to the client on their own field. And there is a quiet red thread from growing food to building a tool that helps families with their food budget.
